Cities

The population density of our cities has a significant influence on quality of life. Population density is simply a measure of the number of people per unit area. There are costs and benefits for constructing cities with either high or low each population density. A city with low population density, enables residents to have yards. But it also means people need to drive places because amenities are often further away. Higher population density generally results in a greater selection of restaurants, bars, cafes, and cultural amenities closer to someone's house. But even within cities, population density is generally extremely varied. This map shows all the cities in the US with a population greater than 50,000.

State and County level populations

Surprisingly, population is only loosely correlated with a state's geographic area. Larger western states are often are often sparsely populated compared to more densely populated eastern states. This interactive map changes from state level to county level as you zoom in so that you can get a closer view of how populations are spread out across the US as a whole and within each state.
